# Atlas Iris — Primary Monogram Logo Specification

Adopted 2026-07-13. Rendered masters live next to this file:
`atlas-iris-logo-dark.png` (navy logo → light backgrounds),
`atlas-iris-logo-light.png` (white logo → dark backgrounds),
`atlas-iris-logo-icon-1024.png` (app icon).

## Overall concept

The logo is a minimalist monogram that subtly combines the letters **A** and
**I** into one continuous geometric mark. It should not immediately read as
"AI". It first appears as a clean abstract symbol; the hidden A and I become
apparent on a closer look.

Same design philosophy as the FedEx hidden arrow or the Amazon smile: simple
at first glance, clever upon inspection.

## Brand feeling

The logo communicates: precision, confidence, premium quality, Scandinavian
minimalism, structure, growth, intelligence, modern software company.

Avoid looking futuristic, cyberpunk, robotic, or overly "AI". Think Linear,
Stripe, Vercel, Notion — not Nvidia or OpenAI.

## Geometry — three integrated elements

### 1. The A

An open geometric triangle, architectural rather than typographic — **not** a
normal font glyph:

- Very sharp apex, no crossbar
- Thick uniform stroke, wide stance
- Bottom-left leg extends slightly lower than expected
- Interior remains completely open

### 2. The diagonal stroke (the most important element)

Begins near the lower-left of the A and rises diagonally through the center.
It is not a line placed on top — it is the **structural connection** between
the A and the I, one continuous gesture. It represents forward motion, growth,
progress, momentum.

### 3. The I

Grows naturally out of the diagonal stroke:

- Tall and elegant, slightly narrower than the A
- Modern serif-inspired feet, clean vertical proportions
- Same visual weight as the A, but never dominating

The viewer should perceive **one symbol** — not "A + I".

## Color

| Element | Color |
|---------|-------|
| A | Deep navy, ~`#0B2A52` (Atlas Iris navy family). Flat — no gradient. |
| I | Bright Atlas Iris blue, subtle vertical gradient `#4F8DFF` (top) → `#1F5FD8` (bottom). No gloss. |
| Diagonal stroke | Same blue as the I; visually transitions into it. |

## Composition

- Fits inside a square; balanced visually, not mathematically centered.
- The A carries roughly 60–65% of the visual weight, the I 35–40%.
- Never any visual separation between the three elements.

## Scalability

Must stay recognizable at 16×16 favicon, 32×32 app icon, 64×64 profile image,
500×500 PNG, business cards, website navbar, building signage. No tiny
details; everything crisp.

## Design philosophy

The viewer's experience: *"That's a clean, premium symbol." → "Wait…" →
"That's actually an A…" → "…and the diagonal becomes the I."* That delayed
recognition is intentional — the logo rewards attention rather than explaining
itself.

## What to avoid

Rounded shapes, circles, eyes, targets, robot aesthetics, AI-chip motifs,
hexagons, excessive gradients, 3D effects, shadows, glow, overly futuristic
styling.

The logo should feel timeless and elegant, capable of representing Atlas Iris
for the next 10+ years.
